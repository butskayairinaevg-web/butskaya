Scatter Diagram Correlation Types

Visual analysis of relationships between paired variables using polished glassmorphism UI

Strong Positive Correlation

Example: Motorcycle speed vs number of accidents – as speed increases, accident rate increases sharply.

Strong Negative Correlation

Example: Machine maintenance time vs breakdowns – more maintenance leads to fewer failures.

Weak Positive Correlation

Example: Advertising spend vs sales – general upward trend, but with high scatter.

Weak Negative Correlation

Example: Price discount vs competitor sales – small downward trend with a lot of variation.

No / Zero Correlation

Example: Shoe size vs exam marks – points are scattered with no visible pattern.

Scatter Diagram – Uses & Benefits

Scatter diagrams help evaluate the direction and strength of correlation between two quantitative variables for decision‑making in quality and statistics.[web:2][web:3]

  • • Quality control: Relate process inputs (temperature, pressure, speed) to defect rates or output quality.[web:3]
  • • Engineering: Study stress vs strain or load vs displacement relationships.[web:3][web:11]
  • • Business & healthcare: Explore links such as marketing spend vs sales or patient age vs recovery time.[web:4][web:18]

Scatter Diagram vs Fishbone Diagram

Scatter and fishbone diagrams are both used in root‑cause and quality work but focus on different aspects of the problem.[web:3][web:21]

Aspect Scatter Diagram Fishbone Diagram
Primary purpose Show strength and direction of correlation between two variables. Organize and categorize possible causes of a specific effect or problem.
Correlation shown? Yes – pattern may be strong positive, strong negative, weak, or none.[web:9] No – displays hypotheses about causes without numerical correlation.
Data type Quantitative paired data (X, Y measurements).[web:3] Qualitative ideas from brainstorming and experience.[web:3]
Typical output Scatter of points with visible trend and possible line of best fit.[web:11][web:14] “Fishbone” diagram listing grouped causes such as Man, Machine, Method, Material.
