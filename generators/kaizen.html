📘 Kaizen – Continuous Improvement

1. Definition

👉 Kaizen means “Change for the Better” or “Continuous Improvement.”
It is a method where small, regular improvements are made in work, process, or daily life.
Word “kaizen”, where “kai” = change “zen” = good, simply means “change for better”.

2. Main Idea

  • Small steps → Big results
  • Improvement is never-ending
  • Everyone in the team contributes (from CEO to workers)
  • Focus on improving processes, not just product

3. Philosophy of Kaizen

  • Respect people → every idea is valuable
  • Everything can be improved → no process is perfect
  • Process = Important → good process gives good results
  • Teamwork & discipline → success is collective

4. Where Kaizen Is Used

  • Manufacturing (factories, assembly lines)
  • Healthcare (hospitals, patient care)
  • Software (development, testing)
  • Services (banks, logistics, customer support)
  • Personal Life (daily routines, study habits)

👉 Kaizen is universal – useful everywhere.

5. How Kaizen Works (Cycle)

It uses PDCA cycle:

Plan
Do
Check
Act
  • 1. Plan → Find problem & make plan
  • 2. Do → Try solution
  • 3. Check → See result
  • 4. Act → Standardize if successful / improve more if not

👉 Repeat this cycle again and again.

6. Kaizen Tools

  • 5S → Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain
  • PDCA → Plan, Do, Check, Act
  • Kanban → Visual board for workflow
  • JIT (Just in Time) → Reduce waste, deliver on time
  • TQM (Total Quality Management) → Everyone responsible for quality
  • TPM (Total Productive Maintenance) → Care of machines & equipment

7. History

  • Origin: Japan after World War II
  • Company: Toyota started using it
  • Inspired by: American quality experts (Deming, Juran)
  • Spread: Now used worldwide in all industries

8. Key Takeaways

  • ✔ Kaizen = Continuous Improvement
  • ✔ Small changes every day → Big improvements over time
  • ✔ Involves everyone in the organization
  • ✔ Focus on process + people for better results
  • ✔ Works in factories, offices, hospitals, IT, and daily life

📌 Simple Example

• In factory → Worker suggests rearranging tools → saves 10 minutes daily → big time saved in a year.
• In personal life → You study 10 minutes extra daily → in a year you complete many extra topics.
