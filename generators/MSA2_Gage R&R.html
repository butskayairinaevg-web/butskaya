01 · DEFINITION

1. Definition

  • Gage R&R is the combined estimate of Repeatability and Reproducibility errors in a measurement system.
  • It helps determine how much variation comes from the measurement system itself.
02 · PURPOSE

2. Purpose

  • To check whether the measurement system is capable (acceptable) or not.
  • To ensure that collected measurement data is reliable for decision-making.
03 · FOCUS OF GRR

3. Focus of GRR

  • Repeatability → Variation when the same operator measures the same part multiple times with the same gage.
  • Reproducibility → Variation when different operators measure the same part with the same gage.
  • Together, they represent the precision of the measurement system.
04 · TYPES OF STUDY

4. Types of Gage R&R Studies

1. Variable Gage R&R Study

  • Based on continuous data (e.g., length, width, diameter, thickness, weight).
  • Methods: Average & Range Method, ANOVA Method.

2. Attribute Gage R&R Study

  • Based on discrete data (e.g., Go/No-Go, OK/Not OK, Pass/Fail, visual inspection).
  • More subjective, depends on inspector’s judgement.
05 · WHY GRR

5. Why Conduct Gage R&R Study?

  • Variation is natural in manufacturing; every measurement system also adds variation.
  • Gage R&R study helps to:
    • Quantify measurement system variation.
    • Identify sources of error (equipment or operator).
    • Improve data reliability.
    • Support tools like SPC, MSA, FMEA, and Six Sigma.
06 · COMPONENTS

6. Components of a Measurement System

  • Personnel (operator/appraiser)
  • Equipment/Gage/Instrument
  • Methods and Procedures
  • Environment (temperature, humidity, vibration, lighting, etc.)
  • Software used for measurement data
07 · GAUGE CONTRIBUTION

7. Gage R&R Contribution

  • Total Variation = Process Variation + Measurement System Variation
  • Gage R&R tells how much of the total variation is due to:
    • Repeatability (equipment variation)
    • Reproducibility (appraiser variation)
08 · BENEFITS

8. Benefits of Gage R&R Study

  • Predicts % of error in the measurement system.
  • Identifies main sources of error.
  • Improves measurement data quality.
  • Reduces wrong decision-making.
  • Supports quality improvement tools.
09 · PROCEDURE

9. Step-by-Step Procedure for Conducting a Gage R&R Study

Preparation

  • Select the measurement equipment for study.
  • Ensure calibration is up to date.

Procedure

  1. Select Appraisers
    • Choose 2–3 normal users of the gage.
  2. Select Parts
    • Choose 10 or more parts representing the full range of process variation.
  3. Number the Parts
    • Label parts (P1, P2, …, Pn).
    • Keep numbers hidden from appraisers to avoid bias.
  4. First Measurement Round
    • Appraiser A measures all parts in random order. Record results.
  5. Other Appraisers
    • Appraiser B and C repeat the same process, without seeing others’ results.
  6. Repeat Measurements
    • Each appraiser measures all parts 2–3 times in different random orders.
  7. Record and Organize Data
    • Enter results in Gage R&R data sheet.
    • Calculate averages and ranges for each appraiser.
  8. Calculate Variations
    • Repeatability = within appraiser variation.
    • Reproducibility = between appraiser variation.
    • Gage R&R = Repeatability + Reproducibility.
    • Part Variation = natural variation among selected parts.
    • Total Variation = √ (Gage R&R² + Part Variation²).
    • ndc (Number of Distinct Categories) = (Part Variation ÷ Gage R&R) × 1.41
  9. Analyze Results
    • Use Average & Range charts to check consistency.
    • Review %Gage R&R and ndc values to judge acceptability.
Strong measurement system → low %Gage R&R (≤ 10%) and high ndc (≥ 5).
10 · ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

10. Acceptance Criteria

%Gage R&R (of Total Variation)

  • ≤ 10% – Acceptable (Good system)
  • 10–30% – Marginal (May need improvement)
  • ≥ 30% – Not acceptable (Needs improvement)

Good measurement system should also have ndc ≥ 5 distinct categories.

11 · ATTRIBUTE GRR

11. Attribute Gage R&R Study

  • Used for attribute (discrete) data such as Go/No-Go, OK/Not OK, Pass/Fail, or visual inspection.
  • Appraisers inspect the same parts multiple times.
  • Agreement is measured in 3 ways:
    1. Appraiser vs Standard – Operator vs known reference value.
    2. Appraiser vs Appraiser – Consistency between operators.
    3. Appraiser vs Self – Operator consistency on repeated trials.

Acceptance Criteria for Attribute Gage R&R

  • ≥ 90% agreement – Acceptable
  • 80–90% agreement – Marginal (training required)
  • < 80% agreement – Not acceptable

14 · SUMMARY

14. Summary

  • Variable Gage R&R → continuous data (e.g., length, weight).
  • Attribute Gage R&R → discrete data (e.g., OK/Not OK).
  • Purpose → quantify measurement error and improve reliability.
  • Acceptability → %Gage R&R ≤ 10% good, ndc ≥ 5 good.
In short: Gage R&R is a powerful MSA tool that confirms whether your measurement system is reliable enough to support critical quality decisions.
