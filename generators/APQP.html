What is APQP?

APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning) is an important tool in product development management and one of the five core tools of the IATF 16949:2016 standard. It provides a complete roadmap for designing and developing products and processes that meet customer needs and expectations, helping industries—especially automotive—ensure quality, performance, and customer satisfaction through effective planning.

Why is APQP Important?

  • Innovation
  • More complex Product
  • Reduce new product development time
  • Complicated supply chain activity
  • Increase in customer and quality requirements

The APQP method helps achieve benefits such as avoiding late changes in product and process, ensuring first-time-right quality, reducing cost, and minimizing risks at an early stage.

Objectives of APQP

  • Ensure clear communication among all involved.
  • Complete all required steps on time.
  • Reduce or avoid quality problems.
  • Ensure good product and process performance.
  • Meet customer needs and expectations.

How to Implement APQP Process

The APQP cycle follows PDCA (Plan–Do–Check–Act) and has 5 phases, 4 major activities, and over 20 supporting tools with feedback and corrective action mechanisms. Key tools: DFMEA, PFMEA, CTQ, Special Characteristics, Control Plan, SPC.

Fundamentals of APQP

Management assigns a responsible person and forms a Cross-Functional Team (CFT) including Marketing, Engineering, Quality, Purchasing, Packaging, and Customer Representatives. Define project scope including customer voice, responsibilities, and quality/cost/delivery impacts. Start with a Product Quality Plan linking concept, experience, and milestones to meet expectations. Formula: Right Leader + Strong Team + Clear Scope + Quality Plan + Proper Timing = Successful APQP.

APQP Phases, Inputs & Outputs

Phase 1 – Plan & Define Program

Objective: Understand customer’s needs and expectations.

Inputs

  • Voice of Customer (VOC), market research, warranty & quality info, lessons learned, business plan, benchmark data, product reliability studies.
  • Customer inputs: identification, traceability, special characteristics, packaging requirements.

Outputs

  • Design, reliability & quality goals, preliminary BOM, process flow chart, special characteristics list, Product Quality Assurance Plan, management support.

Phase 2 – Product Design & Development

Objective: Develop design features into final form ensuring customer satisfaction.

  • Inputs: All outputs from Phase 1.
  • Outputs: DFMEA, DFMA/DFA, prototype & testing, prototype control plan, engineering drawings/specs, material specs, tooling & gauge requirements, confirmed special characteristics, team feasibility approval.

Phase 3 – Process Design & Development

Objective: Establish a manufacturing system ensuring customer needs are met.

  • Inputs: Outputs from Phase 2 (DFMEA, prototype, specs, tooling, gauges).
  • Outputs: PFD, PFMEA, Pre-launch Control Plan, work instructions, packaging standards, layout, MSA plan, capability plan, error proofing, feedback methods, data for reliability and maintainability, quality system review, gateway approval.

Phase 4 – Product & Process Validation

Objective: Validate manufacturing process through production trials and verify product meets requirements.

  • Outputs: Production trial run, control plan, MSA results, capability studies (Cp & Cpk), PPAP, validation testing, packaging evaluation, management approval.

Phase 5 – Feedback, Assessment & Corrective Action

Objective: Evaluate effectiveness of all phases for continual improvement and customer satisfaction.

  • Outputs: Reduced variations, improved satisfaction, better delivery, lessons learned, corrective actions, ongoing partnerships, problem-solving participation.

Key Elements of APQP

  • Cross-Functional Teamwork (CFT)
  • Customer-Focused Quality Planning
  • Risk Analysis (DFMEA and PFMEA)
  • Product and Process Validation
  • Control Plan Development
  • Production Part Approval Process (PPAP)

Benefits of APQP

  • Increases customer satisfaction
  • Early identification of required changes
  • Reduces process variation
  • Ensures on-time delivery of quality products
  • Improves performance and delivery
  • Promotes reliable product design
  • Enhances supplier confidence
  • Controls process changes
  • Supports defect-free launches
  • Maximizes ROI
  • Minimizes waste and non-conformance cost
