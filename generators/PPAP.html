Overview:

PPAP is a key quality core tool in the manufacturing industry, especially automotive, used to establish confidence in suppliers and their manufacturing processes.

It is a documentation process to create and submit required documents to the customer during the new product development stage.

One important PPAP document is Checking Aids, which provides a list of all measurement equipment used in inspections.

Relation to APQP:

PPAP is a critical core tool of IATF 16949:2016 and is triggered in Phase 4 – Product and Process Validation of the APQP process.

Other IATF 16949 core tools include:

  • APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning)
  • FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)
  • MSA (Measurement System Analysis)
  • SPC (Statistical Process Control)

Scope:

Originally, PPAP was used primarily in automotive and aerospace industries.

Now, it is applied in non-automotive industries as well to improve product and process quality, enhance communication, and increase customer satisfaction.

PPAP documentation applies to production and service commodities, including bulk materials. For bulk, raw, or indirect materials, the requirement for PPAP is decided by the customer.

When PPAP Submission is Required:

PPAP is generally required before the first production shipment in the following situations:

  • New product development
  • Changes or corrections to previously submitted products
  • Product modifications due to Engineering Change Notices (ECN)
  • Production using new or modified dies, molds, tools, or patterns
  • Changes in manufacturing methods or processes
  • Changes in machines or raw materials
  • Changes in subcontractor or sub-supplier parts, materials, or services
  • Production tooling or machinery inactive for 12 months or more

PPAP Elements / Documents:

Total of 18 documents, including Checking Aids, design records, DFMEA, PFMEA, control plans, process flow charts, and test results.

Benefits:

  • Confirms that production parts meet customer requirements
  • Reduces risks of non-conformance after product launch
  • Establishes supplier process reliability and capability
  • Supports defect-free product launches

Checking Aids in PPAP

Definition:
Tools, fixtures, gauges, or equipment used to measure parts, verify tolerances, and ensure products meet customer specifications. They support PPAP submission by confirming part quality.

Types:

  • Gauges – Go/No-Go, profile, height, thread gauges
  • Measurement Devices – Micrometer, Vernier Caliper, CMM
  • Fixtures – Hold parts for accurate inspection
  • Visual Aids – Inspection templates, shadow boards
  • Test Equipment – Functional testing of assemblies

Importance:

  • Ensures accuracy and precision
  • Maintains consistent quality
  • Prevents errors in inspection
  • Provides documentation and traceability

Steps to Create Checking Aids Document:

Understand design record (drawing)

List all part dimensions and special characteristic

Compile data in a standardized checking aids template

✅ Steps to Prepare:

  1. List all checking aids used in your inspection process.
  2. Assign a unique ID to each aid/tool/fixture.
  3. Record the specification or dimension that each checking aid verifies.
  4. Add calibration details (date, due date, certificate reference).
  5. Mention usage frequency (per lot, daily, weekly, etc.).
  6. Keep remarks column for audit or special notes.

What Other Documents Do Checking Aids Support in PPAP?

Checking aids are not standalone—they are directly linked with several other PPAP documents. Their role ensures that measurements, inspection, and control methods remain consistent across the PPAP submission package.

  1. Dimensional Results
    Checking aids are used to verify and measure all dimensions listed in the Dimensional Results sheet. They ensure that the part dimensions match customer drawings and tolerances.
  2. Measurement System Analysis (MSA)
    All checking aids (gauges, fixtures, measuring instruments) must undergo MSA studies. The same checking aids listed in PPAP should appear in the MSA Plan and Reports.
  3. Layout Inspection Report
    A Layout Inspection verifies all part dimensions as per drawing. The checking aids mentioned in the PPAP document should also be used for layout inspection, ensuring consistency.
  4. Control Plan
    The Control Plan defines how product and process characteristics will be measured. The measurement methods and instruments listed in the Checking Aids Document must match the ones in the Control Plan.

✅ Audit Relevance:

During Process Audits or Layered Process Audits (LPA), auditors often cross-check the consistency between Checking Aids, Dimensional Results, Layout Inspection, and Control Plan.

Any mismatch may lead to audit findings or PPAP rejection.

Conclusion

Checking Aids Document is an important part of the PPAP package.

It serves as evidence of which inspection methods and measuring devices are used to qualify the part.

This document must be submitted along with other PPAP requirements and approved by the customer.

PPAP Submission Levels

Level 1 – Basic Submission
Part Submission Warrant (PSW) only. Appearance Approval Report (AAR) included if required.

Level 2 – Limited Submission
PSW with product samples. Limited supporting documents as requested by the customer.

Level 3 – Full Submission (Default Level)
PSW with product samples. Complete supporting documentation package. ✅ This is the default submission level for PPAP unless the customer specifies otherwise.

Level 4 – Customer-Specific Submission
PSW along with other specific requirements defined by the customer. Content depends on customer request.

Level 5 – On-Site Submission
PSW with product samples. Complete supporting documents retained at supplier’s location. Documents must be available for customer review at any time.

👉 Note: If the customer does not define a PPAP submission level, Level 3 is followed as the standard/default requirement.

PPAP Documents | PPAP Elements

PPAP has 18 documents as per IATF 16949:2016 requirements and all of these are listed below:

1 Design Records

Customer’s part drawing or specification.

2 Engineering Change Note

Any authorized engineering change documents not yet updated in the design record but implemented in the part.

3 Customer Engineering Approval

Formal approval from customer engineering (if required).

4 DFMEA

Design Failure Mode and Effects Analysis – identifies design risks.

5 PFD

Process Flow Diagram – shows the sequence of manufacturing steps.

6 PFMEA

Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis – identifies process risks.

7 Control Plan

Defines control methods for product and process characteristics.

8 MSA

Measurement System Analysis (e.g., Gage R&R studies).

9 Dimensional Results

Product Layout Inspection results as per customer-approved drawing.

10 Performance Test Reports

Test results for parts with color, grain, or surface requirements.

11 Initial Process Capability Studies

SPC Studies to demonstrate process stability and capability.

12 Qualified Laboratory Documentation

Evidence that testing is done in a certified/qualified lab.

13 Appearance Approval Report (AAR)

Required for parts with appearance requirements.

14 Production Sample Parts

Actual sample parts submitted to customer for validation.

15 Master Sample

A sample part signed off by customer, retained as reference.

16 Checking Aids

Gauges, fixtures, and instruments used for inspection/testing.

17 Customer-Specific Requirements

Any additional documents required by the customer.

18 Part Submission Warrant (PSW)

Summary document of the entire PPAP submission.

PPAP Process Requirements

  1. Significant Production Run
    Parts submitted for PPAP must be produced during a significant production run.

This run should be:

  • 1 hour to 8 hours, or
  • One full shift of production, and
  • With a minimum of 300 parts (unless otherwise defined by the authorized customer quality representative).

Production Conditions

Production parts must be manufactured at the actual production site. They must use the same tooling, gauging, process, materials, and operators as in normal mass production.

Process Control Requirements

During the production trial run: The Control Plan and Process Flow Diagram must be available at the production site. These documents must be strictly followed to meet customer requirements.

Timing in APQP

The PPAP production trial run is conducted in the fourth phase of APQP (Process Validation Phase). The purpose is to validate that the process is stable, repeatable, and capable of producing parts consistently.

👉 Summary: A significant production run is a key requirement in PPAP. It proves that the supplier can consistently manufacture customer-approved parts under actual production conditions, using defined processes and controls.

PPAP – Benefits

  • Better Quality of Products and Services — Ensures that products meet customer specifications and performance requirements.
  • Enhanced Customer Satisfaction — Reliable quality builds customer trust and long-term relationships.
  • Cost Reduction — Minimises costs from poor quality, rework, warranty claims, and product returns.
  • Improved Communication — Strengthens collaboration and clarity between internal teams (design, production, quality) and external customers.
  • Reduced Risk of Failure — By validating processes early, PPAP reduces the chances of failure during the APQP process.

PPAP vs APQP

APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning): A structured framework for planning and defining how a product will be developed and produced. Covers the entire product lifecycle (design → process development → validation → production).

PPAP (Production Part Approval Process): A subset of APQP, focused on ensuring that the manufacturing process produces parts that meet customer requirements consistently. Acts as the evidence of APQP effectiveness.

Relationship between PPAP and APQP

PPAP is an output of APQP. If PPAP fails to meet customer requirements → it indicates that APQP was not implemented effectively. The true test of both PPAP and APQP is during the production trial run. If non-conformances or defects appear in finished product samples, the Cross-Functional Team (CFT) must investigate root causes and close the gaps in both processes.
